Charity help for stray animals in Isparta, Turkey.

About Us

Our homeless and stray animals care fund was created in Isparta on the basis of the volunteer movement for the protection of animals, which has its history since 2012.

Our organization was founded on the belief that animals are entitled to kind and respectful treatment at the hands of humans and must be protected under the law.

What We do

The main goal of the fund is to help homeless animals and the shelters in which they live. Helping vulnerable animals and keeping pets in safe and loving homes requires a commitment from all of us—advocates, pet owners, shelters, leaders, and entire communities.

When we work together under a common cause, we’re both saving lives and elevating our society and its laws to ensure cruelty victims and other at-risk animals receive the protection and care they deserve.

What can You do

If you want to help animals, then on our site you will find many options for how to do this. We will always be happy to welcome new assistants, volunteers and partners.

How else can you help them

Give food and water

Even just feeding a homeless person means giving him a chance to see a new day.

Find shelter

Find caring people ready to shelter the animal for a while, while they are looking for a permanent home for it.

Attach to the family

You can also do PR - actively disseminate through social networks information about animals that need help.

Take to yourself

The best thing is to take a stray animal to your home.

Become a volunteer

You can become a volunteer in the shelter - to help employees with a walk, cleaning, or financially, if possible.

Tell children and teens

It is important to instill in children a love for animals, to conduct bioethics lessons in schools - this will increase the likelihood that the younger generation will be more compassionate and responsible.

Why helping stray animals is important

Every creature wants to live. Each of us, if desired, has the power to help disadvantaged animals!

Have you ever wondered where the street animals came from? After all, this problem was created by people themselves. Stray animals are former pets or their descendants. This sad fact testifies to human irresponsibility - both ordinary citizens and public authorities.
